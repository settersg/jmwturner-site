## Shipwreck, the Rescue

*J.M.W. Turner R.A., c.1802 — Oil on canvas, 161 × 222 cm*

<div class="gallery-grid">

### The Full Painting
![Shipwreck, the Rescue](/images/SWR%20full%20painting.jpg)
*Shipwreck, the Rescue* — Turner's monumental early sea-piece depicting survivors in a rescue boat battling violent waves.

### The Rescue Boat
![Detail: rescue boat](/images/SWR%20crop%20rescue%20boat.jpg)
Detail of the rescue boat — sailors, mothers, and children huddled together against the deadly elements.

### The Red-Coated Figure
![Detail: red-coated figure](/images/SWR%20crop%20red%20coat.jpg)
The mysterious red-coated figure in the stern — possibly Turner's own self-portrait, wearing his characteristic cravat and velvet waistcoat.

### The Black Hero
![Detail: black figure in rescue boat](/images/SWR%20crop%20black%20man.jpg)
A lone black man portrayed as a hero in 1802 — decades before the abolition of slavery. Turner was an abolitionist; this figure is his symbol of social justice.

### The Madonna Group
![Detail: Madonna and child](/images/SWR%20crop%20madonna.jpg)
A mother and child in the rescue boat — reminiscent of medieval Pietà compositions. Martin Butlin noted: "There is even a Madonna group as in The Shipwreck."

### The Survivors on the Spar
![Detail: three survivors on spar](/images/SWR%20crop%20survivors%20spar.jpg)
Three men adrift on a spar, one with arms raised high to hail the rescue boat. Turner's loose brushwork captures their desperation.

### The Wrecked Ship
![Detail: sinking ship](/images/SWR%20crop%20wrecked%20ship.jpg)
The wrecked vessel in the background — derived from Turner's Wilson Sketchbook, c.1797. "An emblem of the hopelessness of man."

### Brushwork Detail
![Detail: brushwork](/images/SWR%20crop%20brushwork%20boat%203%20web.jpg)
Turner's characteristic unblended brushwork — painted largely in the technique of his watercolours, creating immediate visceral impact.

### The JWT Monogram
![Detail: JWT monogram](/images/SWR%20crop%20JWT%20monogram.jpg)
The three letters J, W, T inscribed into the wet paint on the pier — discovered by Peter Paul Biró. A hidden signature in intaglio.

### The Fingerprint
![Detail: fingerprint in foliage](/images/SWR%20crop%20fingerprint.jpg)
A fingerprint pressed into the wet paint of the tree foliage — discovered by forensic investigator Peter Paul Biró.

### The Label
![Label on stretcher](/images/SWR%20label%20stretcher.jpg)
The original nineteenth-century label found on the stretcher: "Shipwreck, the Rescue, J.M.W. Turner R.A."

### The Trumpeter
![Detail: trumpeter](/images/SWR%20crop%20trumpeter.jpg)
The trumpeter in the rescue boat — compare with the similar figure in Turner's *Wreck of a Transport Ship*.
