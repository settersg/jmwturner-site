Chapter 24

Jockeying or Joking

Excerpt from Art World’s Dirty Little Secret by R. Malcolm Setters / Graham Setters

Jockeying or Joking?

Before long, to Martin’s aggravation, Graham introduced the fingerprint issue again, and the battle resumed with the defenseless Paul Biró on the other side of the country.

Graham: “Why would somebody put a false fingerprint…that’s fraudulent!”

Martin in agreement, “That much is fraudulent.”

Bob became somewhat animated: “That’s indictable; that’s criminal… people go to jail with fingerprint evidence – for life! Executed if they find a fingerprint at the scene where somebody was murdered. There’s no question about it!”

Martin: “Now they want DNA evidence.”

Bob: “No they don’t, because it’s less effective. The fingerprint is the foremost forensic evidence.”

Perhaps to calm Bob, Martin submitted, “Well, alright.”

Slightly defused, Bob continued, “There is a Turner fingerprint there, it’s been corroborated by a top Canadian official in the RCMP.”

Martin attempted to critique the fingerprint comparison. “Well look, if you compare the two, well, here there’s a great pit…”

Graham: “They are the experts.”

Bob: “I don’t even want to look at it, it was Turcotte. He’s studied fingerprints for thirty years, and he said, yes [it is right]!”

Martin: “It was Turcotte who did it for Biró, right – Turcotte who was employed by Biró, right?”

Graham: “He wasn’t employed.”

Martin with a chuckle that inferred privileged knowledge of what was going on behind the scenes, “Well he works with him.”

Bob: “Does he?”

Martin: “Yup!”

Attempting to uncover Martin’s meaning, Bob cooperated, “So, maybe it’s Turcotte?”

Martin was starting to enjoy this fanciful game and snickered, “Yeah, well, I read too many thrillers, I read too many thrillers of corrupt policemen and all the rest of it.”

It was not only the FBI and RCMP that Martin did not trust. He confirmed that even if Scotland Yard supported the fingerprint, he would still not believe it.
